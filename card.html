Aye-aye

An Aye-aye is the worlds largest nocturnal primate

  • Scientific Name: Daubentonia madagascariensis
  • Average Length: 16 in.
  • Average Lifespan: 20-23 years
  • Habitat: Tropical rainforests

Aye-aye is an endangered species of lemur native to Madagascar where many of the locals believe the animal to be magical. They are seen as harbringers of evil and often killed on sight and hung up so that the evil spirit will be carried away by travelers. Aye-ayes have a distinct long narrow middle finger that they use to forage for grubs from within trees after they gnaw trough the bark.
